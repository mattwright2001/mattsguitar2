Changing Open Chords to Barre Chords

Any open chord can also be played as a barre chord. Open chords are named by their root notes just like barre chords are, so to convert an open chord to a barre chord, find the root note on either the Low E or the A strings and play open major chords with the major fingering and minor chords with the minor fingering.

Altering Chords

One of my favorite things to do is to unfret a string and let it ring out open with the rest of the chord. This gives the music a fun, artistic flair.

Suspended Chords

A suspended chord (also know as a sus chord) is when the third note of the chord is ommited and replaced with either a perfect 4th or a major 2nd. I'm not going to in depth with musical theory, but basically this means that you are purposfully making the chord sound "ugly" in order to play a "pretty" sounding chord after it. This gives the music a fun dichotomy.
